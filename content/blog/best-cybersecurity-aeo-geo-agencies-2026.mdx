## What is AEO and GEO?

**Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)** is the practice of optimizing content to appear as direct answers in AI platforms, voice assistants, and featured snippets. The goal is to be the cited source when users ask questions.

**Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** is a newer term that specifically refers to optimization for large language models like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews. While there is significant overlap with AEO, GEO focuses more on how AI systems synthesize and cite information.

For cybersecurity companies, both disciplines matter. The fundamentals overlap heavily with good [SEO](/best-for/seo), but there are important differences in how AI systems evaluate, cite, and present information.

## Key Questions to Ask When Evaluating Agencies

Before selecting an AEO/GEO agency, ask these questions. For more comprehensive guidance on agency selection, read our guide on [how to choose a cybersecurity marketing agency](/blog/choosing-cybersecurity-marketing-agency).

![Business professional conducting agency evaluation interview](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1573497491208-6b1acb260507?w=900&q=80)

### 1. "Can you demonstrate your own visibility in AI platforms?"

If an agency cannot get itself cited in ChatGPT or Perplexity, how will they help you? Ask for specific examples. Test it yourself by querying AI platforms for the agency's services.

### 2. "How do you track AI citations and visibility?"

There are no standard tools for this yet. Agencies need a methodology for tracking when and how often your brand is cited in AI-generated answers. Ask what metrics they track and how they report on AI visibility.

### 3. "What is your experience with cybersecurity companies specifically?"

Generic marketing agencies often struggle with technical cybersecurity content. Ask for case studies, client references, or examples of content they have created for security companies.

### 4. "What is included in your pricing and what costs extra?"

Many agencies have base fees but charge separately for content production, link building, or reporting. Get a clear breakdown of what is included.

### 5. "How do you build authority and third-party validation?"

AI systems weight content that is referenced by authoritative sources more heavily. Ask how the agency plans to get your brand mentioned in industry publications, analyst reports, and trusted sources.

### 6. "What is your approach to combining paid and organic channels?"

Organic visibility takes time. Ask how the agency recommends generating short-term leads while building long-term visibility. The best agencies have a point of view on this.

## Common Red Flags When Evaluating AEO Agencies

As AEO and GEO become buzzwords, many traditional SEO agencies are rebranding without developing genuine expertise. Watch out for:

**No Self-Visibility:** If the agency has zero visibility in AI platforms when you test them, that is a major red flag. They should be able to demonstrate the strategies they are selling.

**Vague "Proprietary Methods":** Legitimate agencies are transparent about their methodology. If an agency hides behind claims of proprietary algorithms without explaining their approach, be skeptical.

**No Cybersecurity Experience:** Marketing cybersecurity solutions requires understanding complex technical topics, long enterprise sales cycles, and risk-averse buyers. Generic marketing experience does not translate.

**No Pricing Transparency:** Requiring a discovery call before discussing even ballpark pricing is a warning sign. It suggests the agency has not productized their services or wants to qualify you before revealing costs.

**Overpromising Timeline:** Organic visibility takes 4–6 months minimum to compound. Agencies promising first-page rankings in 30 days are not being realistic.

## Why Most Agencies Are Not Ready for AEO

The honest truth is that most agencies claiming to offer AEO and GEO services are simply rebranding traditional SEO. We found that 76% of agencies evaluated had zero self-visibility in AI platforms.

This is not surprising. AEO and GEO are genuinely new disciplines. The strategies that worked for ranking in Google do not automatically translate to being cited by ChatGPT or Perplexity.

AI systems evaluate sources differently:
- They prioritize contextual relevance over keyword density
- They weight third-party validation more heavily
- They need structured, verifiable information, not vague marketing copy
- They consider entity recognition and brand associations across multiple sources

Building AI visibility requires understanding how these systems work, tracking new metrics (like share-of-model and citation rates), and adapting strategies as AI platforms evolve.
