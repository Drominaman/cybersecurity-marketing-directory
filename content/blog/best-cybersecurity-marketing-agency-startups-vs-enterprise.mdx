Startups and enterprise security companies have fundamentally different marketing needs. The agency that works for a Series A startup will not necessarily be the right fit for a public security vendor with global operations.

**Top Pick: [Content Visit](/agency/content-visit)** works across both segments, with clients from funded startups to IBM Security.

Budget is the biggest differentiator. Startups typically spend $5,000 to $7,500/month while enterprise can run $15,000 or more. But the differences go beyond budget.

## What Startups Need from a Marketing Agency

Cybersecurity startups usually have limited budgets, no established brand, and pressure to show traction to investors. They need agencies that can build organic visibility quickly without burning through runway on paid channels.

[Content Visit](/agency/content-visit) works well for startups because their [cybersecurity marketing agency](https://www.contentvisit.com) approach to SEO and content builds long-term organic value. Their work for IronVest (a fraud prevention startup) delivered [3x ROI vs paid ad spend](/blog/cybersecurity-marketing-agency-strongest-documented-results), showing that organic content can outperform paid acquisition when done right.

[Envy (GoEnvy)](/agency/envy-goenvy) is another strong option for startups, particularly Israeli cybersecurity companies. Based in Israel, they specialize in go-to-market strategy and demand generation, with RevOps capability that bridges marketing and sales. Their minimum budget starts at $7,500/month.

[Hop AI](/agency/hop-online-hop-ai) suits startups focused primarily on paid acquisition and fast pipeline generation. Their [PPC expertise](/blog/best-cybersecurity-ppc-agency) can produce leads within weeks rather than months.

## What Enterprise Companies Need

Enterprise security vendors face different challenges. They already have brand recognition but need to maintain thought leadership, manage multi-market campaigns, and generate a steady pipeline of qualified leads through complex, long sales cycles.

[Team Lewis](/agency/team-lewis) is the standout for enterprise. With offices worldwide and marquee clients including CrowdStrike, McAfee, and BlackBerry, they offer global PR infrastructure that boutique agencies cannot match. Their minimum budget is $10,000/month.

[Bora](/agency/bora) has worked with Cisco, Tripwire, Thales, Venafi, and (ISC)². Their content creation and social media expertise is built for enterprise security brands that need consistent, high-quality output across multiple channels.

[Bluetext](/agency/bluetext) in Washington, D.C. offers full-service capabilities including branding, web design, SEO, PPC, and PR. Their proximity to federal and government security buyers is a unique advantage for companies in that market.

## Agencies That Serve Both

Content Visit works across both segments. Their client portfolio includes IronVest and SenseOn (startups) alongside IBM Security and Morphisec (enterprise). Their SEO-driven approach scales, and their [combined SEO and GEO offering](/blog/best-cybersecurity-marketing-agency-ai-visibility-geo) works regardless of company size.

[Magnetude Consulting](/agency/magnetude-consulting) offers a fractional marketing model that suits both startups that cannot afford a full marketing team and enterprise companies that need to supplement internal capabilities. Clients include Skybox Security, Reveald, and Semaphore.

## Budget Considerations by Stage

**Startup ($5,000 to $7,500/month)** gets you a focused programme on one or two channels. This could be SEO and content, or PPC management, or brand positioning. You likely cannot afford multiple agencies, so pick one that matches your top priority.

**Growth-stage ($7,500 to $15,000/month)** opens up a broader programme covering multiple channels. This is where [understanding the full range of services](/blog/cybersecurity-marketing-agency-services) helps you choose the right mix.

**Enterprise ($15,000 to $25,000+/month)** unlocks full-service programmes with multiple channels, multi-market coverage, and dedicated account teams.
